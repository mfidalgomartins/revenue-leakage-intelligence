# Methodology

## Analytical Objective

The system measures the gap between expected and realized revenue, then prioritizes accounts by four observable risk dimensions. It is designed for analytical prototyping and portfolio demonstration, not direct production decisioning.

## Data Scope

The pipeline generates deterministic synthetic B2B SaaS data with random seed `42`.

| Table | Grain | Expected scope |
|---|---|---:|
| `customers` | One row per customer | 500 customers |
| `monthly_revenue` | One row per active customer-month | 24 months, January 2024 to December 2025 |
| `product_usage` | One row per customer-month-feature | 500 customers × 24 months × 5 features |

Customer records include segment, industry, region, sales rep, annual contract value, onboarding date, and churn status. Monthly records include contracted MRR, billed MRR, collected MRR, discount, payment status, days to pay, engagement, and an embedded leakage pattern.

## Revenue Reconciliation

```text
discount_leakage = contracted_revenue - billed_revenue
collection_leakage = billed_revenue - collected_revenue
total_leakage = contracted_revenue - collected_revenue
```

The pipeline checks that collected revenue does not exceed billed revenue beyond a 1% tolerance and that customer ARR reconciles to the scorecard.

## Risk Dimensions

### Silent Churn

- Uses the latest three monthly engagement observations.
- Flags an account when engagement slope is below `-2` and average engagement is below `30`.
- Converts low engagement and negative slope into a score bounded from `0` to `100`.

### Gradual Deterioration

- Compares average billed MRR in the first and second halves of each account history.
- Uses a three-month rolling average to estimate direction.
- Flags accounts with MRR change below `-10%` and a negative trend slope.

### Discount Anomalies

- Measures average discount, maximum discount, and discount frequency.
- Flags maximum discounts above `25%`.
- Quantifies rep-level discount impact as contracted MRR minus billed MRR.

### Payment Anomalies

- Measures billed-to-collected conversion, failed payments, partial payments, and days to pay.
- Increases risk as collection rate declines and failed-payment count rises.

## Composite Score

```text
composite_risk_score =
    churn_risk × 0.30
  + deterioration_risk × 0.25
  + discount_risk × 0.20
  + payment_risk × 0.25

revenue_at_risk = annual_contract_value × composite_risk_score / 100
```

| Tier | Score | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Low | 0–20 | Healthy or low-priority account |
| Medium | 20–40 | Monitor and validate drivers |
| High | 40–60 | Intervention candidate |
| Critical | 60–100 | Immediate review |

## Validation

`python scripts/run_pipeline.py` executes 19 checks covering:

- row-count and null sanity;
- billed-versus-collected reconciliation;
- ARR aggregation consistency;
- risk-score bounds and weighted-sum accuracy;
- revenue-at-risk caps;
- date coverage and latest-period completeness;
- customer join coverage.

`python -m pytest -q` covers the core analyzer's input validation, discount impact, collection-rate bounds, and composite-score formula.

## Reproducibility

```bash
python scripts/run_pipeline.py
python scripts/explore_data.py
python scripts/run_analysis.py
python -m pytest -q
```

Generated CSVs, reports, and charts are excluded from Git to keep the repository reviewable. The tracked executive dashboard is regenerated by the pipeline from the same deterministic data.

## Limitations

1. Synthetic patterns do not represent observed customer behavior.
2. Static weights are not empirically calibrated.
3. The score ranks risk signals but does not estimate causal effects.
4. No train/test framework or forward-looking model is included.
5. External signals such as CRM activity, support cases, contract terms, and market conditions are excluded.
